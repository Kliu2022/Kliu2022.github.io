The Solution: Affordable and Clean Energy

These following targets are taken from the official UN Sustainable Development Goals website.

7.1: By 2030, ensure universal access to affordable, reliable and modern energy services.

7.2: By 2030, increase substantially the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix.

7.3: By 2030, double the global rate of improvement in energy efficiency.

7.A: By 2030, enhance international cooperation to facilitate access to clean energy research and technology, including renewable energy, energy efficiency and advanced and cleaner fossil-fuel technology, and promote investment in energy infrastructure and clean energy technology.

7.B: By 2030, expand infrastructure and upgrade technology for supplying modern and sustainable energy services for all in developing countries, in particular least developed countries, small island developing States, and land-locked developing countries, in accordance with their respective programmes of support.

Why Isn't It Sustainable?

As we can see from the data above, our current energy production means, which is heavily fossil-fuel based, causes dangerous pollution levels. Moreover, fossil fuels are carbon-based fuels, made from organic matter undergoing millions of years of intense pressure. Our fossil fuel resources are also being rapidly depleted and it is estimated that fossil fuels must be phased out within this century. While new extraction techniques may last temporarily, the affordability of fossil fuels is bound to increase as the supply dwindles.


Burning fossil fuels releases pollution in the form of carbon dioxide, which poses many adverse effects to human health. This image above shows that when coal is burned for heating in the winter months, Beijing's blue skies disappears into smog. There are many health risks when it comes to pollution: including an increased risk of lung cancer.

The emissions from the combustion of fossil fuels also leads to irreversable damage to our planet. Carbon Dioxide, the major pollutant resulting from this combustion, is a strong greenhouse gas. The image above shows that global warming is causing melting polar ice caps, threatening the habitat of many species such as the polar bear.

In addition to the unsustainable nature of fossil fuels and the adverse affects of pollutants, we must also keep in mind that our energy demands are only rising. Not only is the world's population growing exponentially, but countries are also developing. Therefore, fossil fuels are only a temporary solution for a finite amount of time.




How Bad Really Is Pollution?

In Southern Asia, pollution levels are six times higher than the limit, and pollution exceeds the limit almost everywhere.

How Fast are Polar Ice Caps Melting?

Dangerously Fast. Polar ice caps have melted faster in the past 20 years than in the past 10000.

Are Oil Reserves Noticeably Dwindling?

Certainly. The days of the oil industry finding "easy-oil" are long gone. Recently, we have seen military conflict over oil reserves.



In conclusion, we must reduce our unsustainable dependance on fossil fuels and shift more towards cleaner energy sources. As individuals, it is required that we start to take action before it is too late.


What Can You do?

Saving the environment is a collective effort involving every inhabitant on this planet. Although one person is not going to be able to supply the entire country with solar energy, there is still a lot one can do.

Reduce your energy consumption first

While relying on clean energy may not immediately be practical for a household, reducing your unnecessary energy consumption will still help reduce carbon emmissions. Below are a few of the most effective ways to reduce the energy footprint of your house.

Energy Efficient Lightbulbs: Switching to energy-efficient lightbulbs such as LED lightbulbs reduces your power use and saves you money on your power bill!

Hybrid or electric vehicles: Hybrid and electric vehicles still provide the same convenience of driving to your desination, without the nasty carbon footprint.

Energy-efficient Appliances: Consider using energy-saving features on appliances and purchasing energy-efficient devices. This can end up saving you a lot of money!

Consider installing solar panels for your home

Environmentally friendly and economically smart, solar panels are a great and accessible renewable energy source that can power your home without emitting any pollution at all. A single 5.5ft by 3ft solar panel can power 80 LED lightbulbs for four hours a day, according to www.solarpanelrocks.com.

Advocate clean energy sources to your community

Consider writing to your government about the importance of clean energy, or organizing events in your community to advocate the cause. One person's initiative can do a lot!


Advocate clean energy to your business

Let your employers know about these issues! Businesses can be very power hungry, which means there can be a lot of energy to save. Switching to a renewable energy source can also fit many businesses very well.

Consider installing solar panels for your business

If your business has a large structure or land area which is exposed to sunlight, the easiest way for you to take advantage of affordable and clean energy is installing solar panels. Environmentally friendly and economically smart, solar panels are a great and accessible renewable energy source that can power your business without emitting any pollution at all.
